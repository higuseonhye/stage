# First hire: engineer vs. salesperson

**Context:** Post-MVP, a few paying pilots, ~**$2k MRR**. Two founders: one full-time engineering, one on “everything else.” **One hire this quarter.** Bottleneck is founder capacity—but *which* capacity matters most is the real question.

---

## What we’re actually deciding

We’re not picking “which role is more important.” We’re picking **which unknown is blocking the next $3–5k MRR**: repeatable **GTM execution**, **product / reliability / roadmap**, or **we don’t know yet** (in which case we validate before a full-time hire).

---

## Side-by-side

| | **Engineer first** | **Sales / GTM first** |
|---|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Primary bet** | Pilots will expand if we ship the right fixes/features faster | More qualified conversations → more closes with the product as-is |
| **Who gets relief first** | Coding founder | Non-coding founder (sales, CS, follow-up) |
| **Revenue in 90 days** | Usually **indirect** (retention, expansion, fewer fires) | **Direct** if real pipeline exists |
| **Typical burn** | Higher fixed salary | Often lower base + variable |
| **Ramp to useful** | Often **4–6+ weeks** on codebase | **3–6 weeks** to real pipeline motion (not “fully independent”) |
| **Depends on** | Clear customer-backed priorities; founder can still sell | Product “good enough” for the next 2–3 customers; documented or teachable motion |

---

## Hire-order logic (use this order)

1. **If pilots are blocked on product** (reliability, missing must-have, churn risk): **engineer first**—selling harder into a broken promise burns trust.
2. **If you have multiple warm / qualified opportunities stalling from follow-up, demos, or bandwidth** (not from “no fit”): **sales/GTM first**—founder-led sales hits a structural ceiling without another pair of hands.
3. **If neither is clearly true:** **don’t default to FTE.** Run the one-week validation below (or a fractional ops / fractional eng / short contract) until the signal is obvious.

**Alignment check:** Each founder writes independently: *“If we hire X, what would I finally be able to do that I can’t today?”* Compare answers **before** posting a job—misalignment here kills more early teams than a mediocre hire.

---

## Biggest risks if we pick wrong

| Wrong choice | What goes wrong |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Engineer, but sales was the cap** | Revenue flat; non-coding founder burns out; engineer builds features that don’t move MRR. |
| **Sales, but product was the cap** | New deals churn or stall; engineering founder underwater; reputation damage in a small market. |
| **Either without a playbook** | Quiet failure at 90 days: similar MRR, higher burn, same bottleneck. |

---

## 90-day plan — how each path differs

**Engineer first**

- **Weeks 1–2:** Onboard; 2–3 pilot calls for unfiltered product signal; freeze a **short** roadmap (what next 2–3 pilots need).
- **Weeks 3–8:** Ship 2–3 high-impact items tied to that roadmap; coding founder aims to **reduce** support and context-switching, not disappear.
- **Weeks 9–12:** Non-coding founder runs a **small, structured** outreach wave using a written ICP + story of why pilots bought.
- **Success signal:** Fewer critical bugs, clearer expansion/renewal path from pilots, coding founder **≥10 h/wk** back on product strategy.

**Sales / GTM first**

- **Weeks 1–2:** Pipeline audit (warm, lost, stalled); one-page ICP + “how we actually closed the pilots.”
- **Weeks 3–8:** Steady discovery/calls; **velocity cap** (e.g. 2–3 new customers max in 90 days) so quality stays ahead of promises.
- **Weeks 9–12:** Repeatable steps (CRM, objections, handoff to engineering); measure **conversations → qualified → closed**.
- **Success signal:** Qualified pipeline moving; 1–2 new pilots or clear “why we lost” pattern; coding founder **not** permanently firefighting alone.

**Week 4–6 kill switch (agree now):** If the chosen path shows **no** movement on its core metric (shipped priorities / pipeline conversations), **stop** and reassess—including hiring the complementary role or pausing burn.

---

## One week before we commit (minimum signal)

| Activity | Owner | Output |
|----------|--------|--------|
| 5–10 conversations: pilots + lost/no-decision | Both (split) | One theme: product blocker vs. timing/budget vs. no ICP fit |
| Pipeline truth: names + stage + last touch | Non-coding founder | “We have / don’t have” follow-up debt |
| Time log (real hours, one week) | Both | Where capacity actually goes—sales vs. support vs. ops |

**Decision rule:** Hire the role that **removes the constraint the data shows**. If the data is muddy, **defer FTE** and buy a smaller, reversible slice of capacity first.
